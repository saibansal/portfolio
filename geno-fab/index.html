Neil Gibbons, M.B.A. – CEO of GenoFAB LLC

Neil Gibbons has served in senior operational and strategic capacities in various corporate roles including C-suite positions as a Board Director and Executive Chair. He has served as President and CEO of venture backed SaaS financial services-technology company he co-founded securing over $30m in venture investment and building the organization from start-up to a public company with a $350m market capitalization with listings on both US and European exchanges.

He has significant entrepreneurial start-up experience with early stage ventures, as well as a solid record of accomplishments in a number of demanding turnaround situations in organizations with failing business plans, successfully engineering revitalizations to growth and profitability on behalf of the stakeholders.

His accomplishments include having delivered double-digit growth in revenues and profits from multi-site international operations in Europe, Asia-Pacific, and the USA in consumer products, higher-education management, and financial services technology industries.

Neil Gibbons received his MBA with Distinction and Dean’s List Honors from IMD, Lausanne, Switzerland.

Jean Peccoud, Ph.D. – President and Chief Scientific Officer of GenoFAB LLC

Jean Peccoud is an expert in computational synthetic biology. His current scientific interests include the development of linguistic models of DNA sequences, the optimization of DNA fabrication processes, and the development of new instruments to measure the dynamics of gene networks in live cells. Dr. Peccoud's group is leading the development of GenoCAD, an open source web-based application to design synthetic DNA molecules from libraries of standard genetic parts. In the 1990s, Dr. Peccoud pioneered the development of stochastic models of genetic networks.

Dr. Peccoud joined the Virginia Bioinformatics Institute at Virginia Tech in 2006 as Associate Professor. Prior to joining VBI, he was responsible for a research program at Du Pont focused on gene and regulatory network discovery, the design of DNA transformation vectors, and the development of methods to analyze the genetic properties of gene networks. Dr. Peccoud has been a visiting professor in the department of electrical engineering at the University of Washington, a visiting scholar with Wolfram Research, and the recipient of a NATO Fellowship. He serves as Academic Editor of PLoS ONE.

Dr. Peccoud is a graduate of the University of Joseph Fourier (France), the University of Paris (Orsay, France), and completed his post-doctoral training in Molecular Biology at the University Louis Pasteur (Strasbourg, France).
